Data abstraction and encapsulation.



  The notions of abstraction and encapsulation are commonly used in human-machine interaction and are widely prevalent in the technology-oriented world.
  Consider an example of a DVD player. Here,all our interactions with a DVD player are made through buttons on the control panel such as   PLAY,STOP,PAUSE. The DVD player is packaged so that we cannot directly interact with the circuitry inside. So,the internal representation of a DVD   player is hidden from the user. This is the principle of Encapsulation.

  The instruction manual that accompanies the DVD player tells us what the DVD player is supposed to do if we press a particular button. It does not   tell us how this function is implemented inside the DVD player; it does not,for example, explain the sequence of electronics and mechanical events   that result when the PLAY button is pressed. So,the instruction manual makes a clear distinction between what the DVD player does and how it   does. This distinction is the principle of Abstrction.
